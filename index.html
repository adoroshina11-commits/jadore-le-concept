Dolce & Gabbana
Exhibition,
Milan.

A visual study of craftsmanship, theatricality, and the relationship between fashion and cultural narrative. Each image examines the discipline and precision that underpin the final image of luxury.

The Full Silhouette.

Each look is presented as a complete composition, where structure, styling, and ornament are treated as equally important. What emerges is not just a garment, but a fully realised image shaped through proportion, texture, and detail.

Dolce & Gabbana's visual language is rooted in contrast — tradition and spectacle, discipline and excess. Each piece reflects a deeply Italian narrative, where craftsmanship is not hidden but emphasized, and ornament becomes a form of storytelling. The garments do not simply dress the body; they construct identity, drawing from history, religion, and theatre to create something both timeless and deliberately exaggerated.

Inside Carnival · Venezia

Each winter, Venice shifts from a quiet, fog-covered city into a world of masquerade, ornament, and theatrical fashion. Carnival transforms the canals into a living spectacle where craftsmanship, fantasy, and identity blur together.

About
the
Concept.

Get in Touch →

My first introduction to fashion came long before I understood the industry itself. It lived in conversations, sketches, fabrics, and stories passed down by my mother, who studied fashion and taught me to look closely.

Growing up among five siblings and a family of entrepreneurs, I learned to adapt, create, and approach the world with curiosity. Business taught me structure; art taught me observation.

I collect passions the way others collect objects. Photography, writing, jewelry making, design, fashion, and culture all offer different ways of telling the same story. This space is a reflection of that curiosity — an archive of moments, ideas, and details that might otherwise be forgotten.
